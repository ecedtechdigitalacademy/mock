QUESTION 5

(a) DEFINITION OF A ROBOT

A robot is a machine programmable by a computer that is capable of carrying out complex tasks automatically. OR A robot is a mechanical or virtual artificial agent, typically controlled by a computer program or electronic circuitry. OR A robot is a machine programmable by a computer that is capable of carrying out a complex series of actions automatically.

(2 marks)

ASIMOV'S THREE LAWS OF ROBOTICS

First Law: A robot may not injure a human being, or through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm.

Second Law: A robot must obey the orders given it by human beings, except where such orders would conflict with the First Law.

Third Law: A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Law.

[6 marks @ 2 marks each]

DIFFERENCE BETWEEN VARIABLES AND CONSTANTS

A constant is a named data item with a fixed value, while a variable is a named data item whose value can change during the course of program execution.
